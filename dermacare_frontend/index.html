Understanding Skin Conditions

An overview of all conditions this AI can detect. Run an analysis to see personalised causes, risk factors, and recommended next steps based on your result.

🔴
Inflammatory · Acne

Acne Vulgaris

Acne occurs when hair follicles become clogged with oil and dead skin cells. It appears as whiteheads, blackheads, pimples, or painful cysts — most commonly on the face, chest, and back.

🟠
Allergic · Contact Dermatitis

Contact Dermatitis

An itchy, inflamed rash that appears when skin comes into contact with an irritant or allergen — such as soaps, jewellery, cosmetics, or plants like poison ivy.

🥹
Chronic · Eczema

Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis)

A chronic inflammatory condition causing itchy, red, dry, and cracked skin. It often flares periodically and is closely linked to asthma and hay fever. It is not contagious.

🟢
Autoimmune · Psoriasis

Psoriasis

An autoimmune condition that accelerates the skin cell lifecycle, causing a buildup of cells that forms scales and red patches. It tends to flare and subside cyclically.

🧕
Parasitic · Scabies

Scabies

A highly contagious infestation caused by tiny mites (Sarcoptes scabiei) that burrow into the skin, causing intense itching — especially at night — and a pimple-like rash.

🍄
Fungal · Tinea

Tinea / Ringworm / Fungal Infection

A common fungal skin infection that produces a ring-shaped, scaly rash. Can affect the scalp, body, groin, feet (athlete’s foot), and nails. No actual worm is involved.

🦠
Viral · Warts

Warts & Viral Skin Lesions

Small, rough growths caused by the Human Papillomavirus (HPV). They can appear on hands, feet (plantar warts), face, or genitals and spread by direct skin contact.

🔮
Oncology · akiec

Actinic Keratosis / Bowen’s Disease

A rough, scaly patch caused by years of sun exposure. Considered a precancerous lesion — it can progress to squamous cell carcinoma if left untreated.

🧬
Oncology · bcc

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

The most common form of skin cancer. It grows slowly and rarely spreads, but can cause significant local damage if untreated. Typically appears as a pearly or waxy bump.

🦄
Benign · bkl

Benign Keratosis-like Lesions

Includes seborrheic keratoses, solar lentigines, and lichen-planus-like keratoses — all benign (non-cancerous) skin growths appearing as waxy, scaly, or flat brown patches.

🔵
Benign · df

Dermatofibroma (DF)

A common, benign skin growth that usually appears on the legs as a small, firm bump. Often pink-brown in colour and slightly itchy. They are harmless and do not become cancerous.

🟡
Bacterial · Impetigo

Impetigo

A highly contagious bacterial skin infection mainly affecting young children. It appears as red sores that rupture quickly, ooze fluid, and then form a yellowish-brown crust.

⚠️
Oncology · mel

Melanoma

The most serious form of skin cancer, originating in melanocytes. It can spread to other organs rapidly. Early detection is life-saving — check moles using the ABCDE rule regularly.

✅
Healthy · Normal Skin

Normal Skin

No significant skin condition detected. Normal skin has a balanced moisture level, an even tone, small pores, and minimal sensitivity — the body’s first line of defence.

🔵
Benign · nv

Melanocytic Nevi (Common Mole)

Commonly known as moles — benign growths of melanocytes. Most moles are harmless, but any change in size, shape, or colour warrants prompt medical attention.

🩸
Vascular · vasc

Vascular Lesions

Abnormalities of the blood vessels appearing on or near the skin — including cherry angiomas, spider angiomas, port-wine stains, and pyogenic granulomas.

🤚

This tool cannot replace a doctor

DermaScan uses a machine learning model trained on labelled skin image datasets. While it identifies visual patterns, AI models can make mistakes — particularly with poor lighting, unusual angles, early-stage conditions, or rare presentations. No AI tool should be used to self-diagnose or self-treat any skin condition. Always seek the advice of a qualified dermatologist or medical professional.
